Meghalaya NEET PG Counselling: A Guide to the Admission Procedure

 

The National Board of Examinations (NBME) administers the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET), a national-level test for admission to postgraduate medical programmes in India. For admission to MD/MS/Diploma/DM/MCh programmes in government, private, deemed universities, and private medical schools in India, NEET PG is a single window test.

 

One of the Indian states that provides postgraduate medical education is Meghalaya. The North Eastern Indira Gandhi Regional Institute of Health and Medical Sciences (NEIGRIHMS) is the only government-run medical school in Meghalaya. A range of postgraduate medical programmes are available at NEIGRIHMS.

 

eligibility requirements

 

Candidates must fulfil the requirements listed below in order to be eligible for NEET PG counselling in Meghalaya:

 

They must have succeeded on the MBBS/BDS test from an accredited institution.

They had to have received an overall grade of at least 50% (45% for candidates with disabilities and 40% for SC/ST candidates).

As a medical graduate, they must have completed a year of internship.

They need a legitimate NEET PG score.

Application Method

 

The Directorate of Health Services, Government of Meghalaya website is where applicants for NEET PG counselling in that state submit their applications. From March 1 through April 30, 2023, the NEET PG application form will be accessible on the website of the Directorate of Health Services.

 

For NEET PG counselling in Meghalaya, the application fee is 1,800 INR for general/OBC applicants and 1,200 INR for SC/ST students. Online payment options for the application fee include debit, credit, and net banking.

 

Award List

 

The candidate's NEET PG score and selected specialisation will be used to create the merit list for NEET PG counselling in Meghalaya. Following the NEET PG test, the merit list will be made available on the website of the Directorate of Health Services.

 

a seat assignment

 

The Directorate of Health Services' online counselling mechanism will be used to allocate seats for NEET PG counselling in Meghalaya. Based on the applicants' NEET PG scores, specialisation preferences, and category, NEIGRIHMS will assign them a place in a postgraduate medical programme.

 

Significant Dates

 

Exam date for the 2023 NEET PG: September 9

Result of NEET PG 2023: October 20, 2023

Meghalaya NEET PG counselling 2023: November 2023

Conclusion

 

A transparent and equitable method of allocating seats in postgraduate medical programmes at NEIGRIHMS is via NEET PG counselling in Meghalaya. To continue their postgraduate medical education, students may have to make compromises on their chosen specialisation or go to other states owing to the restricted number of postgraduate medical seats in Meghalaya.

 

Additional Details

 

Around 150 people are enrolled in postgraduate medical programmes in Meghalaya.

The cut-off for Meghalaya's NEET PG counselling fluctuates from year to year. But 600 is generally considered to be the cutoff.

There are two phases to the counselling procedure for Meghalaya's Directorate of Health Services. State quota seats are determined in the first round, while All India Quota (AIQ) seats are determined in the second round.

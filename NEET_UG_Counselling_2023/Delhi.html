Everything You Need to Know About NEET UG in Delhi

 

For undergraduate medical and dentistry programmes in India, there is a nationwide admission test called the nationwide Eligibility cum admission Test (NEET). The Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MoHFW) administers the NEET UG, which has a Delhi state quota.

 

 

Everything you need to know about the NEET UG Delhi test, including the eligibility requirements, exam format, curriculum, and key dates, will be covered in this blog article. We'll also provide some advice on how to study for the test.

 

eligibility requirements

 

You must fulfil the requirements listed below in order to register for the NEET UG Delhi exam:

 

You must have earned a passing grade in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB) in order to continue.

A minimum of 50% overall in your 12th standard is required.

To study in India, you must be an Indian citizen or a visitor with a current visa.

The NEET UG test must have been cleared by you.

Exam Format

 

The NEET UG Delhi test has 720 questions and lasts for three hours. There are two parts on the test:

 

Section I: Chemistry and Physics (360 points)

Biology Section II (360 points)

Multiple-choice questions (MCQs) make up the exam's questions. Every incorrect answer has a mark deduction of one.

 

Syllabus

 

The 11th and 12th grade NCERT textbooks served as the foundation for the NEET UG Delhi exam's curriculum. The following subjects are covered in the syllabus:

 

Chemistry: Inorganic Chemistry, Organic Chemistry, and Physical Chemistry Physics: Mechanics, Electromagnetism, Modern Physics, and Optics

Morphology, Physiology, Genetics, Microbiology, Biochemistry, and Pharmacology are all branches of biology.

Significant Dates

 

The following are the crucial dates for the NEET UG Delhi:

 

Opening of the application period: April 2023

Application period expires in May 2023.

Date of admissions card release: June 2023

the July 2023 exam

Date of announcement of results: August 2023

Tips for NEET UG Delhi Preparation

 

Given how tough the NEET UG Delhi test is, early preparation is crucial. Here are some pointers for getting ready for the test:

 

Get acquainted with the course curriculum first.

Create a study plan and follow it.

To determine your progress, take practise exams.

To assist you with your preparation, consider joining a study group or hiring a tutor.

On the test day, maintain your composure and attention.

Conclusion

 

Although the NEET UG Delhi test is difficult, it is not insurmountable. You may fulfil your ambition of studying medicine in Delhi with effort and commitment.
